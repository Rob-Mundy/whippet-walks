Useful Training Tips



Ahead of your first pack outing it is important that your four-legged-friend is able to walk on the lead and has good recall in case of an emergency.

We have compiled several videos below that can assit with the training as it can be very frustrating at times! Dogs respond most effectively to consistency and repetition, and some breeds such as sighthounds can take longer than others to learn a new behaviour.

Persevere and you will get there! Good luck!

How to walk your dog on the lead

Video source: Zak George's Dog Training Revolution on YouTube

Ensuring your dog walks nicely on the lead is essential training for any pup and is best taught from a young age, though can be introduced later if you have adopted a rescue.

The primary reason for training your dog to walk nicely on the lead is for their safety. Dogs are much more likely to injure themselves if they are out of control and lurching at everything they pass. Owners are also more prone to injuries when walking unruly pets. Leash pulling in particular can cause rotator cuff injuries in the elderly, and it is more likely that an owner will drop the lead which could result in the dog taking off. Ultimately an untrained dog will become frustrated and potentially reactive when on the leash.

Consistent leash walking provides a regular, safe mode of exercise for you and your pooch, enhancing your bond, and establishing your dominance.

How To teach your puppy to heel

Video source: Zak George's Dog Training Revolution on YouTube

By training your dog to heel you are improving their leash manners and self-control, and creating a much more pleasant walking experience for you and your pet.

Heel training ensure your dog remains close to your left hand which is free to dispense treats when the pooch is compliant therefore allowing for more control. It serves as an effective tool for navigating potentially problematic scenarios such as approaching nervous or reactive dogs, crossing busy roads, or traversing awkward terrain.

Add heel training to your pet obedience arsenal and enjoy pleasantly well-mannered walks with your pooch!

How to work on your dog's recall

Video source: Zak George's Dog Training Revolution on YouTube

"Come!" is arguably the most important command you can teach your dog. Having good recall, and the ability to return to you when instructed is essential if you are planning to allow your dog off the lead.

This can be challenging for young pups and certain easily distracted breeds, and equally frustrating for owners, but when successfully taught is an invaluable tool for maintaining control over your dog. Distractions for dogs are varied and numerous: other dogs, animals, balls, cyclists etc., so being able to disrupt a dog's inquisitiveness or desire to chase and have them return to you on command is powerful and can often avert a dangerous scenario such as approaching a reactive or aggressive breed.

This one is definitely worth persevering with and is a prerequisite for attending any of our pack walks. Good luck!

How to get your dog's attention

Video source: Zak George's Dog Training Revolution on YouTube

Another great tool to get your dog's attention is the "watch me" or "look at me" command. In a similar way to practising heel or recall training, it provides yet another way to distract your dog from whatever it is they're doing and focuses them on you.

You will often find yourself in situations wherby it is necessary to interrupt your dog's behaviours, such as digging the lawn, rooting through a bin or generally getting too excitable. A pup that is trained to look its owner in the eyes and maintain eye contact can be immediately disarmed in any situation, which is particularly useful when out and about, or when trying to teach a new command.

As with all training, these commands need to be practised in a number of situations, initially indoors in a safe environment, then onto areas containing distractions. Once mastered, you will have yourself a happy and obedient pooch!
